Aware, Module 01

What Is a
Digital Footprint?

Every action you take online leaves data behind. Your digital footprint is the trail of information you create, share, and leave exposed, whether you know it or not.

The Three Types

Understanding your
digital trail

Not all digital footprints are created the same way. Understanding the difference is the foundation of awareness.

Active
What You Choose to Share
Social media posts, online forms, profile information, and anything you deliberately publish. You control this, but most people share far more than they intend to.
Passive
Collected Without You Knowing
Browsing history, location data, device information, and behavioral data collected silently in the background. You did not choose to share this. It was taken.
Persistent
Stays Even After Deletion
Cached pages, data broker records, and information already sold to third parties. Deletion does not equal erasure. Data can outlive your account by years.
Exposure Sources

Where your
data comes from

01

Location Services

Your movements tracked each time an app accesses your GPS. Many continue tracking in the background when not in use.

02

Apps and Platforms

The average smartphone has 80 or more apps. Many request access far beyond what they need to function.

03

Wi-Fi Connections

The networks you join reveal your location patterns and browsing behavior. Public Wi-Fi is particularly risky.

04

Search History

Every query builds a profile of your interests, sold to advertisers and retained indefinitely.

05

Data Brokers

Third-party trackers follow you across websites and compile personal information legally, without your knowledge.

Biometric Data

Protect what cannot
be changed.

Your face, iris, voice, and behavioral patterns are unique identifiers that, unlike passwords, cannot be reset if compromised.

Face
Facial Recognition
Used in retail surveillance and law enforcement. Your facial structure is a permanent identifier.
Behavior
Behavioral Patterns
How you type, scroll, and move through apps creates a fingerprint used to identify and track you.
Voice
Voice Recognition
Your voiceprint can identify you instantly. Smart speakers and phone systems capture it continuously.
Iris
Iris Patterns
The patterns in your eyes are entirely unique and increasingly used in access control and identity verification.
