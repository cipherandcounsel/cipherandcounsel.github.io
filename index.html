Digital Footprint Awareness

Cyber, simplified.

Cybersecurity should not feel out of reach. Understanding your digital presence is the first step to protecting it. Most cyber threats rely on human behavior - not advanced technology.

01
What is a digital footprint?
Every action you take online leaves data behind - social media activity, websites you visit, apps you download, information you share. Two types exist: active footprints, what you choose to share, and passive footprints, what is collected about you without your direct awareness. Your data can be tracked, stored, and used - often without your knowledge.
02
What is phishing?
Phishing is when attackers pretend to be trusted sources to trick you into sharing sensitive information. This includes fake emails from banks, messages asking for passwords, and links that look real but are not. If something feels urgent or suspicious - pause. Verify before you click. Most cyber threats succeed because of human behavior, not technical sophistication.
03
Public WiFi risks
When you connect to public WiFi and check your email or log into social media, that network may not be secure. Your data could be exposed in seconds. Use a VPN when on public networks. Avoid logging into sensitive accounts - banking, email, healthcare - on any network you do not control. Small, smart decisions make a measurable difference.
04
3 ways to protect your footprint today
Turn on two-factor authentication on every account that supports it. Review your social media privacy settings and limit what is publicly visible. Stop reusing passwords - a password manager makes this manageable. Security starts with awareness. These three steps take less than 30 minutes and significantly reduce your exposure.
