Mission Statement

Why Cipher & Counsel exists

```

Most people do not understand or actively manage their digital footprint - leaving them vulnerable to privacy, security, and legal risks. Cipher & Counsel was built to change that. Through education, practical guidance, and advocacy, this platform makes cybersecurity understandable and actionable for everyone - while building pathways for women to lead in cybersecurity, policy, and digital law.

Security does not stay inside a network. It shows up in boardrooms, courtrooms, and policy hearings where the people making decisions rarely speak the language of the practitioners who briefed them. That disconnect is where the real damage happens.

My degree in Cybersecurity with a Minor in Public Policy and Law is a deliberate commitment to working at that junction - because the practitioners who can translate technical risk into decisions that stick are the ones who move the needle.

As an undergraduate student in the UTSA Honors College, I founded Cipher & Counsel as a platform whose name reflects its purpose: the convergence of cyber and law. The vision is larger than a portfolio. It is an online community built to raise digital footprint awareness and develop the next generation of women working in cybersecurity, policy, and law.

Building that as an undergraduate student, from a laptop, using publicly available sources and academic rigor, is the standard I hold myself to.
```
