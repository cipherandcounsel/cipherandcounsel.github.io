Security does not stay inside a network. It shows up in boardrooms, courtrooms, and policy hearings where the people making decisions rarely speak the language of the practitioners who briefed them. That disconnect is where the real damage happens. My degree in Cybersecurity with a Minor in Public Policy and Law is a deliberate commitment to working at that junction.

As an undergraduate student in the UTSA Honors College, I founded Cipher and Counsel as a platform whose name reflects its purpose: the convergence of cyber and law. The vision is larger than a portfolio. It is an online community built to raise digital footprint awareness and develop the next generation of women working in cybersecurity, policy, and law.
