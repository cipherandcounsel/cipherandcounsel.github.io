Protect — Module 02

Simple Steps.
Major Impact.

Protecting your digital footprint does not require technical expertise. It requires awareness and a few deliberate habits. Lock it. Protect it. Respect it. Own it.

Highest Impact Actions

Take control of
your digital presence

These six actions reduce your exposure by the greatest amount with the least effort. Do these first.

01

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

2FA blocks 99% of automated account attacks, even when your password is compromised. Enable on every important account: email, banking, social media in priority order. Use an authenticator app over SMS when possible.

02

Use a Password Manager

Stop reusing passwords. A password manager generates and stores unique, strong passwords for every account. Bitwarden is free. 1Password and Apple Keychain are excellent alternatives.

03

Review Your Privacy Settings

Settings are frequently reset after app updates. On your phone: Settings, then Privacy, then review every app permission. On social media: limit ad tracking and restrict public visibility.

04

Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi

Public networks are shared. Without a VPN, others on the same network can potentially see your unencrypted traffic. Avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi.

05

Clean Up Dormant Accounts

Every account you have ever created is a potential entry point. Check haveibeenpwned.com for breach exposure. Delete accounts you no longer use.

06

Think Before You Click

Phishing emails now use AI to mimic trusted sources perfectly. Never click links in emails. Go directly to websites by typing the address yourself. If something feels urgent or suspicious, pause.

Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA is
non-negotiable.

A strong password is no longer enough. Multi-factor authentication adds a second layer of verification that prevents unauthorized access even when your credentials are stolen.

Fingerprint. SMS code. Authenticator app. Pick any two. Block 99% of attacks instantly.

Daily
  • Think before clicking
  • Lock devices
  • Log out public devices
Weekly
  • Check app updates
  • Review permissions
  • Scan login alerts
Monthly
  • Review privacy settings
  • Check breach databases
  • Delete unused apps
Physical Security

Lock down your
devices. Literally.

Physical security is the first line of defense against data breaches. A stolen unlocked device is a total compromise. Stolen devices can expose your personal and business data even when digital protections are in place.

Secure today. Safe tomorrow.
