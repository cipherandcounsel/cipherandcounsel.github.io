Protect — Module 02

Simple Steps.
Major Impact.

Protecting your digital footprint does not require technical expertise. It requires awareness and deliberate habits. Lock it. Protect it. Respect it. Own it.

Highest Impact Actions

Take control of
your digital presence

01

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

2FA blocks 99% of automated attacks. Enable on email, banking, and social media. Use an authenticator app over SMS when possible.

02

Use a Password Manager

Stop reusing passwords. A password manager generates and stores unique, strong passwords for every account. Bitwarden is free.

03

Review Your Privacy Settings

Settings reset after app updates. Review every app permission on your phone and restrict social media visibility regularly.

04

Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi

Public networks are shared. Avoid logging into sensitive accounts without a VPN on public networks.

05

Clean Up Dormant Accounts

Every old account is a potential entry point. Check haveibeenpwned.com and delete what you no longer use.

06

Think Before You Click

AI-generated phishing emails now perfectly mimic trusted sources. Go directly to websites by typing the address yourself.

Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA is
non-negotiable.

A strong password is no longer enough. Multi-factor authentication adds the protection that blocks 99% of automated attacks — even when your credentials are already compromised.

Fingerprint. SMS code. Authenticator app. Pick any two. The difference between a secure account and a breached one is one extra step.

Your Security Ecosystem

Protection is
layered, not single.

Effective digital security is not one tool or one habit. It is a layered ecosystem — strong passwords, MFA, privacy settings, network awareness, and deliberate digital habits working together.

Daily
  • Think before clicking
  • Lock devices when idle
  • Log out on shared devices
Weekly
  • Check app updates
  • Review permissions
  • Scan login alerts
Monthly
  • Review privacy settings
  • Check breach databases
  • Delete unused apps

Strong habits offline

lead to stronger security online.
